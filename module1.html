Introduction to Internet Safety


The internet can be an exciting and useful tool, but it also comes with risks. Without knowing how to protect yourself, you may become a target for scammers, hackers, or identity thieves. Internet safety involves practices that help protect your personal information, secure your devices, and avoid dangerous situations online.

In this section, we'll introduce you to the basics of internet safety. You'll learn about common online risks and why it's important to protect yourself while browsing the web.

Remember, just like locking your door at home, securing your online presence is an important step in keeping you safe.

3/6



Basic Internet Security

    To keep your personal information safe, it’s important to practice basic security steps when you’re online. These steps include:
  1. Strong Passwords: A strong password is key to protecting your accounts. Use passwords that are hard to guess by combining uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Try to avoid using names or common words.

  2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Many websites offer two-factor authentication, which adds an extra layer of protection. After entering your password, you’ll also need to verify your identity using a second method, like a code sent to your phone.

  3. Updating Software: Always keep your computer, phone, and apps up-to-date. Updates often include important security fixes that protect you from hackers and malware.

  4. Secure Websites: Before entering personal information like passwords or credit card numbers, make sure the website is secure. Look for a small padlock symbol next to the website address (URL), and ensure it starts with “https://” instead of just “http://”.

4/6



Phishing Scams and Fraud

Phishing is a type of scam where criminals attempt to trick you into revealing personal information like passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers. These scams often come in the form of fake emails, texts, or phone calls. Here’s how to recognize and avoid them:


  1. Suspicious Emails or Texts: Be cautious of unsolicited emails or texts that ask for personal information. If an email claims to be from a company or bank you trust but looks suspicious, do not click any links or open attachments.

  2. Phony Offers: Scammers may promise you prizes, gifts, or financial help. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Avoid responding to offers that pressure you to act quickly or ask for money upfront.

  3. Spot the Red Flags: Look out for strange email addresses, poor grammar, or urgent requests for sensitive information. Legitimate companies rarely ask for such information via email.



5/6



The Dangers of Public Wi-Fi and How to Protect Yourself

Public Wi-Fi is convenient, but it can also be a security risk. When you connect to a public Wi-Fi network, hackers may be able to intercept your data, like passwords or personal details. Here’s how to stay safe on public Wi-Fi:


  1. Avoid Sensitive Transactions: Never enter sensitive information, such as your bank account details or credit card numbers, while connected to public Wi-Fi. Use cellular data or a secure network for these activities instead.

  2. Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network): A VPN encrypts your data, making it more difficult

  3. Turn Off Sharing: When using public Wi-Fi, turn off any file or device sharing features on your phone or computer to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.

By following these tips, you can reduce the chances of falling victim to cyberattacks on public Wi-Fi.



6/6



Keeping Personal Information Safe Online

Your personal information is valuable, so it's important to protect it. Here are some key tips to keep your information safe online:

  1. Don’t Overshare on Social Media: Avoid posting too much personal information on social media platforms. Details like your birthday, home address, or travel plans can be used by criminals to steal your identity.

  2. Protect Your Devices with Passwords: Always use a password or PIN to lock your phone or computer. This adds an extra layer of security in case your device is lost or stolen.

  3. Use Password Managers: A password manager can securely store and organize your passwords, making it easier to use strong and unique passwords for each account.
