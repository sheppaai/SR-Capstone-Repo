Introduction to Safe Browsing


Browsing the internet can be fun and informative, but it also exposes you to risks. Safe browsing means navigating the web in a way that minimizes your risk of encountering scams, viruses, or malware.


In this section, we’ll teach you the basics of safe browsing. By the end of this page, you’ll know how to protect yourself while searching for information, shopping, or connecting with others online.

  2. Spot pop-up ads and links that could lead to scams or malware.

  4. Use browsers with built-in security features.
  5. Recognize warning signs that a website might not be safe to visit.

3/6



Recognizing Safe Websites


Not all websites are trustworthy. Here’s how to tell if a website is safe:

  1. Check the URL: Look for a URL that starts with “https://” (the “s” stands for secure). Also, make sure there is a padlock symbol next to the URL. These signs mean that the site is secure and uses encryption to protect your data.

  2. Look for Trust Signals: Reputable websites often display security badges or certifications. If you’re unsure, research the website by looking for reviews or checking its reputation.

  3. Avoid Unknown or Suspicious URLs: If a website’s address is difficult to read or seems off, it could be a scam. For example, misspelled domain names or unfamiliar suffixes (e.g., .xyz, .club) may indicate a dangerous site.

4/6



How to Avoid Malware and Viruses


Malware is malicious software designed to harm your device or steal your information. Here’s how to protect yourself:

  1. Download Only from Trusted Sources: Avoid downloading files or programs from unfamiliar websites. Stick to trusted sources like official app stores (Apple App Store, Google Play) or well-known software providers.

  2. Beware of Pop-Up Ads: Pop-up ads can sometimes trick you into downloading harmful software. Always close pop-ups without clicking on them, especially if they promise rewards or free products.

  3. Install Antivirus Software: Antivirus programs can help detect and remove malware from your device. Make sure to install antivirus software and keep it updated to protect against new threats.


5/6



Safe Online Shopping and Banking


When shopping or banking online, it’s crucial to ensure your financial information is safe. Here’s how:

  1. Check for Secure Payment Methods: Only use secure, well-known payment methods, such as credit cards or trusted payment services (PayPal). These often provide extra protection if something goes wrong.

  2. Look for Secure Checkout Pages: Before entering payment details, check for a padlock symbol and “https://” in the website’s URL. These signs indicate a secure connection.

  3. Review Bank and Credit Card Statements Regularly: Regularly check your statements for any unauthorized transactions. Contact your bank immediately if you notice anything suspicious.

6/6


Browsing Social Media Safely


Social media can be fun, but it’s important to stay safe. Here’s how to protect yourself while browsing:

  1. Adjust Privacy Settings: Ensure your social media profiles are set to private. This way, only people you know can see your posts and personal details.

  2. Be Careful What You Share: Avoid sharing too much personal information on social media, such as your location, phone number, or vacation plans. Criminals can use this info to target you.

  3. Report Suspicious Accounts or Messages: If you receive strange or suspicious messages, or if you spot fake accounts, report them to the platform. This helps keep the community safe.
