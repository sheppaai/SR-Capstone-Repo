3/6



Creating a password




A strong password is about 12 characters long, with a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
-symbols include any of the following: ~`! @#$%^&*()_-+={[}]|\:;"'<,>.?/
Passwords should be unique and hard to guess






4/6



Using different passwords


It's important to use a different password for every account you set up so that if one password is stolen, your other accounts are still protected.
Below are some more helpful reasons on why you should use different passwords:
Prevents Identity Theft: If someone gets into your accounts with the same password, they could steal your identity, open new accounts in your name, or misuse your details.
Keeps Online Shopping Safe: Many websites store your payment information. If a hacker gets into one of those accounts, they could steal your money if you use the same password elsewhere.




5/6



What to do if you forget your password


Don't panic if you forget your password. Most websites have easy ways to help you get back into your account.
Look for a “forgot password” link. This will guide you through the recovery process and typically emails you to reset your password
Answer security questions when creating accounts
-sometimes when you forget your password, you can answer security questions to get into your account -these questions may include things like your mother’s maiden name or the name of your first pet





6/6



Remembering and storing passwords


Below are a few helpful tips to remember passwords and how to safely store them.
Avoid Writing Them in Obvious Places: Don’t keep your passwords on sticky notes stuck to your computer or phone. Someone could easily find them.
Avoid Keeping It in Your Wallet or Purse: Don’t store your password list in your wallet or purse, because if it's lost or stolen, someone could access your accounts.
If you write your passwords on paper, try storing them in locked drawers or safes.
